Creating Your Bot:

In order to make it easy to get started, we have sample bots bellow which are already set up and simply need to be uploaded to Heroku or another hosting service. We currently only have sample bots for Python and Javascript with more on the way!

Bot Requierments

When creating your bot, there must be an endpoint `/bet`. This is the endpoint that Probot Playground will call in order to communicate with your bot. This endpoint must be a direct child to your URL. For a guide on setting up the endpoint be sure to check out the sample bots above.

You are free to handle the request from Probot Playground however you would like, however, your bot must respond within five seconds. Failure to respond in time will result in your bot folding its current hand. If your bot errors when called the current hand will also be folded.

Game Tables

Joining Tables

Currently, you may join tables through the lobby page once logged into your account. When choosing a table you may choose the stakes (small/big blind) of the table as well as your buy-in. Each stake level has a different accepted buy-in range, with buy-ins getting larger with larger stakes.

Leaving Tables

In order to leave a table, your bot must respond with a bet of -1. You will then be qued to leave once the current hand finishes. If your bot reaches zero chips while at a table, it will be kicked from the table. You must then rejoin a table. This is currently only do able through the lobby page, however we will be introducing an endpoint to automate joining tables as well.

Game Data:

The data your bot receives will be a JSON object consisting of a ‘state’ and ‘history’ attribute. The ‘state’ is a JSON object of the current state of the game. The ‘history’ is an array of JSON objects representing all the past states of the current hand.

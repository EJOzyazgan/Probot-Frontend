No-Limit Texas Hold 'Em

Overview:

In No-Limit Texas hold'em players try to make the best five-card poker hand according to traditional poker rankings (shown below). Each player is dealt two cards face down (the "hole cards"), then over the course of subsequent rounds five more cards are eventually dealt face up in the middle of the table. These face-up cards are called the "community cards" because each player uses them to make a five-card poker hand.

The five community cards are dealt in three stages: Flop, Turn, and River. (details below)

Players construct their five-card poker hands using the best available five cards out of the seven total cards (the two hole cards and the five community cards). This can be done by using both of the hole cards in combination with three community cards, one hole card in combination with four community cards or no hole cards and playing all five community cards — whatever works to make the best five-card hand.

If the betting causes all but one player to fold, the lone remaining player wins the pot without having to show any cards. For that reason, players don't always have to hold the best hand to win the pot. It's always possible a player can "bluff" and get others to fold better hands. If two or more players make it all of the way to the showdown after the last community card is dealt and all betting is complete, then the only way to win the pot is to have the highest-ranking five-card poker hand.

Hand Ranks:

Betting:

Betting begins to the left of the dealer button and continues in a clockwise direction around the table. The dealer button is shifted one to the left with each hand played. The player with the dealer button does not deal the cards, this is simply used to determine who will pay big and small blinds. The player to the immediate left of the dealer pays the small blind and the next respective player the big blind.

The player with the small blind is always the first to bet each round and the player with the dealer button is always last to bet. Betting continues until all but one player has folded, or all five community cards have been placed.

Betting Options

  • Check: Bet no chips and continue to the next player. Only applicable if the current minimum bet is zero.
  • Minimum Bet (Call): Equal to the current largest bet on the table.
  • Minimum Raise: If raiing must raise as much as the previous bet or raise in the same round. As an example, if the first player to act bets $5 then the second player must raise a minimum of $5 (total bet of $10).
  • Maximum Raise: The size of your stack (your chips on the table).
  • Fold: Forfeit the current hand as well as all current bets.

There are up to four betting rounds per hand: Pre-flop, Flop, Turn, and River. The Pre-flop round takes place after all players receive their two cards, there are no community cards in this round. After the Pre-flop the dealer will burn (discard) one card and then place down three community cards starting the Flop round. The Turn and River rounds follow respectively with one card being burned and one community card being shown in each round. The River round has all five community cards shown and is the final round of betting for the hand.

A showdown occurs at the end of each hand after the River. This is when all still active players show their hands one by one in a clockwise direction beginning with the last player who bet or raised. The player with the best hand wins the current bot and the next hand begins.

If two or more players have the same ranked hand, then the extra cards are taken into account. These cards are called “Kickers” and are the cards each player has that are not used to make their current ranking. The player with the highest Kicker wins the hand.

The Blinds:

Blind rules depend on whether it is a tournament game or not. In tournament games, the blinds will gradually get larger as the game progresses. In non-tournament games, the blinds will stay constant.

The amount of each blind is in relationship to the table buy-in with the small blind generally being half of the big blind. Tables generally are marked by their blinds, for example, 1/2, 2/4, 4/8, etc. Although tables are marked by blinds, the buy-in amount allowed at each table is also in relation to blinds. Tables with smaller blinds will have lower limits for buy-ins vs tables with larger blinds.
